Front Row

Front Row is a social media platform which allows users to share their concert memories with their friends and the world. Instead of seeing each of your friends' footage from the same tour separately, Front Row splices your friends' memories together into one cohesive swipe through concert story. Or view all the highlights of a concert through a single crowdsourced concert story from the artist themself. Whatever your style, Front Row allows you to experience music live, remotely.

Features

See Concerts from a New Perspective

Relive preformances of the past and present through crowsourced preformance footage that covers each of the most anticipated and exciting moments from the event. See shows your friends are going to and tours your favorite artists are currently on.

Friends Feed

Swipe up for a new concert story from a friend or right to view more footage from the same post.

Artists Feed

Swipe up for a new tour story or right to view more footage from the same story.

Comment and Like

Interact with posts from your friends and favorite artists by commenting and liking.

Capture and Organize Your Concert Memories

Take videos and photos, then string them together into a single concert story so your memories are saved forever. The footage you upload could appear in the artist's crowdsourced tour story.

Personal Profile

View your archive of concert stories, as well as your upcoming concerts.

Adding Footage to Story

Add photos or videos to an existing story or add it to a new story.

Inside a Concert Story

Add friends who went to the concert with you to create a group concert story. All your memories are ready to be looked back on!

Unlimited Concerts at Your Fingertips

Watch preformances from friends, venues, or artists on their profiles. All the music highlights from the past and present can be streamed on demand.

Friends Profiles

Check out what shows your friends are going to, and what artists they already heard live.

Venues Profiles

See what concerts your local venue is hosting, and what artists they've hosted in the past.

Artists Profiles

See what tours artists are currently on, and what tours they've preformed in the past.

Explore and Purchase Tickets

Discover which new artists, fellow music lovers, and your local venues.

Explore Page

Discover which artists are touring near you, users you might know, and new venues in your area.

Tour Info Page

See the setlist for a tour and listen to it on Spotify or Apple Music. Then, compare ticket prices amongst sellers and purchase your seat.

Stay Updated

Get notifications about your profile and when your followed artists are touring near you.

The Process

The Problem

How can music lovers post their concert videos online in a way that allows outside viewers to experience the concert in more true-to-life, engaging manner?

Using Other Apps as Examples

I began solving this problem by looking at inspiration on both sides of the user experience quality spectrum. I examined VividSeats, a popular ticket purchasing application, and GigIt!, a rather underdeveloped concert video sharing application. I used what I learned from this research to gather postive and negative aspects about each app so that I could start to mold my solution to the problem.

Branding Guide

I then decided on the typeface hierarchy and color pallette for the interface. I decided that making the app dark mode would help create a concert-like feel throughout the experience. I used a lively purple for buttons to complement the black and stand out amongst the variety of colors that typically appear within concert footage.
