Our Space

For spaces with more than 3 roommates trying to coexist, Our Space allows roommates to create a rotating chore chart and shared calendar, write posts airing out grievances (or positivity), and pay/message their landlords or roommates. Instead of scattering this functionality across Paypal, Google Calendar, and messaging apps, Our Space offers a seamless experience that will enhance the relationships amongst roommates. Designed for mobile and web.

Communicate Effectively with Your Roommates

On your home page, stay updated on household announcments and discussions through your news feed. Roommates can also post anonymously about issues within the house should they want to air out a grievance without consequence.

Delegate Chores, Randomly

Once you add all the chores you need done to your household, the tasks are assigned proportionally to roommates through random draw. Less conflict, cleaner surroundings.

Share a Household Calendar

Before having that Friday night party in the communal living room, create an event to let your roommates know in advance. With a shared calendar, everyone is on the same page.

Pay Rent and IOU's

In a click, pay recurring or one time expenses to any of your roommates or landlord. Recurring expenses allow roommates to create an expense for each of their bills.

Messaging with Convenience

Chat conveniently with your landlord and roommates within Our Space.

The Process

The Problem

How can roommate communication be simplified through the use of technology, considering the technology that roommates currently are using?

Crafting User Personas

To better understand the struggles of my user population, I interviewed people currently living in with roommates. Though I had experience living with roommates already, I wanted to see what trends in the interview responses would appear and how communication changes based on the size of the living space, number of roommates, characteristics of the roommates, or other unforeseen factors.

Site Map

I created a site map to plan out the various pages within Our Space and ensure I covered of each step in the user workflows. The main sections of the site are the household news feed, calendar, pay, and messaging.

Wireframes and User Testing

Using the site map, I designed grayscale wireframes with Balsamiq. The lo-fi renderings helped translate my ideas to the page, even if I wasn't 100 percent sure about where each element should go. It was also extremely helpful in making sure I had all the screens necessary for the workflows.

Benchmarking Other Sites

In order to better understand how to sequence my workflows and design my final UI, I needed to benchmark. I looked at Facebook's group page functionality to see how I might go about designing Our Space's roommate-facing homepage. I also studied Google Calendar to see how I might create the calendar. Lastly, I looked at Google Sheets because it seemed to be a popular way to create a chore chart amongst the roommates I interviewed at the start of the project. For each site, I made notes about the positive and negative aspects of my experience while completing the workflows.
