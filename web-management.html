Web Management

In Summer 2019, I redesigned the website for Figable, a mobile app aimed at teaching children fiscal responsiblity and positive character. During that summer, I also began working for Language University, a foreign language education company. As the digital content manager, I have created a new iteration of the company's website to meet their demand for digital programs and helped to grow their social media presence.

Language University Website Redesign

At the start of the project, Language University had a website that felt somewhat clunky and was not particularly designed for mobile. In an effort to the modernize the Squarespace website, I began by investigating the type heirarchy, reducing its size and changing the typefaces to reflect the playful nature of the brand. I removed the border that took unnecessary screen space and created a consistent styling for linked text and photos. I layed out each page to help customers complete the most common workflows: Registration for a program, navigation to social media channels, and general interest in learning more about the company and its offerings.

LU Landing Page

The landing page opens with a clean view of the LU logo, the nav bar, and the company's mascots. As the user scrolls down, they are presented with an introduction to the services of the company and a sample lesson video. Below are cards that link to the most important pages on the website, helping users navigate to the page appropriate for their query. The page ends with embeded displays of the company's Instagram and blog to help advertise for those channels.

Offerings Overview

In the event a user wants a overview of all of Language University's programs, they can find that at this hub. The programs are organized by type and each accompanied by a description, pricing charts, and link to further information.

Digital Program Registration

Each of the major digital services have their own info page with the important details and a video associated with the program. There is a button below the top part of the info page that links to the purchasing page for the program, when the user is ready.

Digital Store

The digital store holds all the individual crafts, books, and activities available for purchase. I updated the store to show four products per line so the user could navigate through items more quickly. I also added a subscription service to the store, which provides customers with monthly lessons for their children. Overall, this part of the project allowed me learn more about setting up products for sale on CMS software.

Language University Social Media Graphics

I also managed Language University's Facebook and Instagram. I posted a mixture of branded graphics to convey important information and video content of the students engaging with the company's classes. This messaging coincided with the website redesign to inform customers of the brand's new digital programs.

Figable Website Redesign

In collaboration with the founder of Figable, Helen Malick, I redesigned the mobile app's website using Wix.com. Using Figable's brand manual for reference, I repurposed the content from the original website into a layout that felt more modern. I also designed a one-pager, explaining the purpose and target users of the mobile app, for use with investors.
